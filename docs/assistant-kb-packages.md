# EveryDayAi — Package FAQ for the voice assistant

This document answers the questions callers most commonly ask that the three package cards on the /services page do not explicitly cover. The voice assistant reads from it to answer follow-up questions accurately.

## Who is Graham?

Graham Blackwell is the founder of EveryDayAi. He spent 20+ years running finance and business systems for Australian construction, civil engineering, and services businesses — including a group turning over around $60 million a year. He is a practical operator rather than an academic, and his advice is grounded in how real businesses actually work day-to-day. He is based in Perth, Western Australia, and works with clients across Australia.

## Where are you based?

Perth, Western Australia. Most engagements run remotely over video calls and shared documents. On-site days are available for discovery sessions and team training, and can be added to any package at cost.

## Can I buy just the AI Readiness Review without committing to anything further?

Yes. The Review is a one-off, fixed-price engagement. You own the written report and can act on it however you choose — in-house, with another provider, or with us. There is no obligation. Most clients do go on to a Sprint on one of the top opportunities the Review identifies, but it is not required.

## What size business do you work with?

Our sweet spot is Australian small-to-mid businesses turning over between $1 million and $50 million. These businesses are established enough to have real workflows worth automating, and small enough that we deal directly with the owner or senior leader — no account-manager layers. We also occasionally work with larger organisations on specific departmental projects, and with sole traders or very small businesses where the fit is right.

## Can the Implementation Sprint cover more than one process?

No. The Sprint is deliberately scoped to one well-defined process at a time. This is what lets us ship a working system in four weeks rather than a slide deck. If you want to automate multiple processes, we run them as back-to-back Sprints or move you onto a Retainer.

## What happens after the Implementation Sprint?

The Sprint includes 30 days of bug-fix support after go-live. Beyond that, you own the code and access and can maintain the system yourself, move onto a Retainer for ongoing improvements, or run ad-hoc work at preferred rates.

## Who owns the code and tools we build?

You do. All code, credentials, and accounts are handed over to you at the end of the Sprint. We are not a reseller and not locked to any vendor. If the best tool for the job is Claude, OpenAI, Google, Zapier, Make, n8n, Supabase, or something custom, that is what we will use. The recommendation is based on fit, not affiliation.

## What industries do you work in?

We are sector-agnostic, but Graham's deepest experience is in construction, civil engineering, professional services, hospitality, and distribution. Any business with real workflows — quoting, invoicing, purchasing, inventory, compliance, reporting, customer service — is a fit.

## Do you sign NDAs?

Yes. Everything shared during any engagement is confidential. We are happy to sign your NDA before the discovery call if you prefer.

## What is the discovery call?

A free 20-minute video call with Graham to understand what you are trying to achieve, decide whether there is a sensible fit, and agree on the next step. No prep required. If it turns out we are not the right people for the job, Graham will tell you that and, where possible, point you at someone who is.

## How quickly can we start?

Typically two to three weeks from signed agreement to kickoff. The Review can sometimes start sooner. For urgent work, Graham will say so on the discovery call.

## Are prices in Australian dollars?

Yes. All prices are AUD, GST-exclusive. Graham is GST-registered (ABN on file), so tax invoices include GST.

## Can we pay in instalments?

Graham is happy to discuss payment terms on a case-by-case basis. He will confirm this with you personally on the callback.

## Do you work with overseas clients?

Primary focus is Australian businesses. International clients are occasionally accepted where the fit is right — raise it on the discovery call.

## What is the difference between the Sprint and the Retainer?

The Sprint is one intensive build in four weeks — pick a process, we deliver it. The Retainer is ongoing light-touch support with one quarterly implementation of up to 20 hours — it suits businesses that want AI embedded in how they operate, not bolted on once.

## Can I combine packages?

Yes. The most common path is: Review first, then a Sprint on the highest-ROI opportunity, then a Retainer to keep compounding. Each step is optional and you decide after you have the report.
